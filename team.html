Our Team

Our coaching staff are extremely passionate about helping you achieve your goals.

At CrossFit Midnight Life we are committed to providing you with the best experience possible. All of our staff take pride in their dedication to health and fitness and are undoubtedly committed to helping you become the best version of yourself.

Come meet our team!

Jade Batten

Owner & Head Coach of CrossFit Midnight Life

Why did you start CrossFit? I spent 10 years in the military and functional, constantly varied exercises that make you better at life always interested me. In 2012 one of my Army mates suggested we go and try out “CrossFit” after work one day. We went to this tiny little box and the workout was a huge number of pullups followed by an 800m run several times. We raced each other the whole way, had a great time and I’ve never looked back.

Favourite thing about CrossFit? This might sound cliché but there is nothing better than something that can train the 10 general physical skills. (cardiovascular/respiratory endurance, stamina, strength, flexibility, power, speed, coordination, agility, balance and accuracy.) Whatever your sport, CrossFit will make you better at it.

Favourite and least favourite movements? I’d have to say I love anything with a barbell and some fast grinding work like burpees and wallballs. My least favourite movements are anything gymnastics but that also makes me love them more.

Gus

Staff

Why did you start CrossFit? It took 18mths for my wife to convince me to really have a crack at CF. I dropped into Jodes affiliate in Sydney a couple of times but hated it. When we moved to Wagga, she found an affiliate and asked me again. This time within two visits I was hooked. Within a month I wanted to become a coach. 6yrs later here we are.

Favourite thing about CrossFit? Hands down the Community. The local affiliate vibe once you settle in can become family like. Who you are outside the box means nothing once you’re next me grinding through the WOD. All respect to you for turning up and pushing through. We all experience the love hate relationship of a WOD together.

Favourite and least favourite movements? Least favourite is the thruster. If you like them you’re a sick human. Favourite movement is the deadlift. It’s simple - bend over, hold on to the barbell, stand up tall, lower and repeat.
